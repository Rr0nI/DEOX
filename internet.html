The right internet tariff for you

If you need help looking for a new Internet tariff , you've come to the right place at CHECK24. As our customer, you not only get the best offers, but also an all-round service.

Read here how DSL, cable or fiber optic tariffs differ and what LTE tariffs are suitable for. One feature that all tariffs have in common is WIFI. With a WLAN router, you can access the Internet via WLAN with any Internet tariff .

DSL tariffs

DSL tariffs are the classic among Internet tariffs. Due to its high availability, almost every household can use DSL for its Internet connection and thus surf the Internet quickly . Depending on the network and provider, DSL tariffs are available with up to 250 Mbit/s. The best-known DSL providers are Telekom , Vodafone , o2 or 1&1 .

cable tariffs

Internet via cable is implemented via the television cable connection and, depending on the network expansion status, delivers gigabit speeds on site . There are cheap offers for cable tariffs in the Internet comparison of CHECK24 from providers such as Vodafone Kabel Deutschland or Vodafone Unitymedia as well as PŸUR .

fiber optic tariffs

Internet via fiber optics is the most modern and future-proof transmission technology . Gigabit speeds of 1,000 Mbit/s and more can easily be offered via fiber optics, and more and more Internet providers are upgrading their networks with fiber optics. The availability of cheap fiber optic tariffs is also increasing accordingly.

LTE

LTE tariffs are mobile phone connections for high-speed Internet, which are primarily suitable for smartphones or tablets. However, LTE can also be used for your Internet at home and offers bandwidths of up to 500 MBit/s . The data volume is very generous for LTE tariffs at home with 200 GB and more.

landline tariffs

Fixed network and Internet tariffs always come together and are the best alternative for making cheap calls and surfing the Internet via a fixed network . Some landline tariffs also include a mobile phone flat rate in addition to an Internet and landline flat rate. Fixed network tariffs are offered via DSL as well as via cable or fiber optics.

Tariffs without a contract period

Internet contracts with a short term of one month or twelve months are few and far between, although the demand for them has increased. The offers that are available for tariffs without a contract period offer maximum flexibility thanks to a notice period of a few weeks , but cost a little more.

Tariffs for young people

Some Internet providers offer special Internet tariffs for young people under the age of 30 or students and trainees . These Internet tariffs for young people are usually cheaper than normal tariffs in terms of the basic fee or include special advantages such as shorter terms or free months . Another advantage can also be a free time-out option , which allows the contract to be paused for a certain period of time, for example during a semester abroad. All the advantages of tariffs for young people are listed on the Internet price comparison.
